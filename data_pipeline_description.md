Presentations you should be ready to give

Technical case study (30–45 min) — a real project you delivered end-to-end, framed as: business problem → solution design → tradeoffs → results → what you'd do differently. Pick something where the data platform work enabled a downstream analytical or AI outcome
AI solution design (live or take-home) — design an AI-enabled solution for a concrete business problem (claims summarisation, agent-assist, underwriting acceleration). Cover data sources, ingestion, model choice, eval, deployment, monitoring, responsible AI controls, cost. One whiteboard slide is enough
Vision / "how I'd approach this role" deck (10–15 min) — your view on what an AI engineering team should own, where you'd invest first, how AI engineering should partner with platform, data, and DevOps. Senior-level loops almost always want this
30 / 60 / 90 day plan — concrete deliverables, learning goals, relationships you'd build, and one tangible win you'd target in the first 90 days
Portfolio / demo walkthrough (5–10 min) — your refreshed RAG or ML demo, with focus on the engineering discipline around the model (eval, observability, deployment) rather than the model itself
Responsible AI mini-talk (informal, be ready if asked) — three slides on how you'd handle data governance, model risk, and transparency for an AI feature, ideally referencing your existing RBAC / audit work as the same muscle

Day-of execution tips

Lead with the data-platform-as-AI-foundation framing in your opening 60 seconds
Use the internal vocabulary of the AI team (mirror their tooling and terminology)
When you don't know something, say so and immediately follow with how you'd learn it — learning agility is explicitly in the JD
Bring at least one strong opinion about where the company's AI work should go, but hold it loosely
Close every interview with a specific next step you'd take in the role
